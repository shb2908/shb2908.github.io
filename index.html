About Me

Hello, I am Soham Bose, an AI researcher and developer with a strong focus on natural language processing, machine learning, and computer vision. I am driven by complex problem solving, enjoy deep exploration of large codebases, and actively engage with challenging engineering problems that require rigorous analytical thinking. Beyond this when I am not coding or brainstorming, I enjoy my time listening to music (huge fanatic of rock), and spend time playing the guitar. I also enjoy reading, and I am drawn to sci-fi and thriller films and delve into complex narratives of it.

Research Interests

My current research agenda investigates how to incentivize the capabilities of pretrained models by empowering them to perform complex reasoning through multimodal learning, interacting with environments, and constructing their own goals. I explore how to make models interpretable so they can properly illustrate the underlying process of their reasoning while moving away from shortcut solutions.

ü§ñ Agentic Reasoning

To make agents able to reason under complex environments especially through coding or embodied approaches and to collaborate among themselves in solving long-horizon goals while maintaining consistency and improving through reflection.

üîó Multi-Modality

To explore how multimodal contexts can act as sources of richer learning signals and make models utilize the best of both modalities for enhanced understanding and reasoning.

üîç Interpretability & Robustness

Understanding how internal representations evolve over time during interaction, adaptation, and learning. Analyzing whether models can maintain stable and meaningful internal states while avoiding biases and spurious correlations.
